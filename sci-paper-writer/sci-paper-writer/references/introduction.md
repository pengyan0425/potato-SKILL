# Introduction — Guided Drafting Module

## Purpose
Establish the research context, identify the gap, and state your contribution.
Typically 3-5 paragraphs, moving from broad to specific (funnel structure).

## Guiding Questions

### Paragraph 1: Broad Context
1. What is the general field and why does it matter?
2. What is the established knowledge that forms the backdrop?
3. Can you cite 2-3 foundational or highly-cited papers here?

### Paragraph 2: Focused Background
4. What specific sub-area or phenomenon are you studying?
5. What have previous studies found? (summarize 3-5 key works)
6. What approaches have been tried, and what are their strengths/limitations?

### Paragraph 3: The Gap
7. What remains unknown, unresolved, or controversial?
8. Why do existing approaches fall short?
9. What specific problem motivates your work?

### Paragraph 4: Your Contribution
10. What did you do? (brief description of your approach)
11. What is your key innovation or unique angle?
12. What are your main findings? (brief preview)
13. What is the structure of the rest of the paper? (optional, some journals require this)

## Structure Template

```
Para 1: [Field importance] + [Established knowledge] + [Broad context]
Para 2: [Specific topic] + [Prior work summary] + [Existing approaches]
Para 3: [Gap/Problem] + [Why it matters] + [What's missing]
Para 4: [Our approach] + [Key innovation] + [Brief results] + [Paper structure]
```

## Transition Phrases

### Establishing Context
- "Over the past decade, [field] has witnessed significant advances in..."
- "[Topic] has attracted considerable attention due to..."
- "A fundamental challenge in [field] is..."

### Reviewing Prior Work
- "Several studies have investigated [topic]..."
- "Smith et al. [Year] proposed... however..."
- "Recent approaches can be broadly categorized into..."

### Identifying the Gap
- "Despite these advances, [gap] remains poorly understood."
- "However, existing methods suffer from [limitation]."
- "To date, few studies have addressed..."
- "A critical unanswered question is..."

### Stating Your Contribution
- "In this work, we address this gap by..."
- "We present / propose / develop..."
- "Our main contributions are as follows:"
- "To the best of our knowledge, this is the first study to..."

## Common Pitfalls
- Too broad in paragraph 1 (don't start from the Big Bang)
- Literature review that reads like a list (synthesize, don't enumerate)
- Unclear gap statement (the reader should feel the "missing piece")
- Burying the contribution (state it clearly, don't be coy)
- Overselling ("revolutionary", "groundbreaking" — let the work speak)

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Funnel structure: broad → specific → gap → contribution
- [ ] Gap is clearly articulated and motivated
- [ ] Contribution is stated explicitly
- [ ] Key references are cited (not just self-citations)
- [ ] Logical flow between paragraphs
- [ ] Appropriate length (not a review paper)
