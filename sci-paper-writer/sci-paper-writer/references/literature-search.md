# Literature Search Guide

## Search Strategy

### Building Effective Queries

1. **Start broad, then narrow**: Begin with 2-3 core concepts, add specificity
2. **Use synonyms**: Include alternative terms for your concepts
3. **Boolean operators**: AND (intersection), OR (union), NOT (exclusion)
4. **Phrase search**: Use quotes for exact phrases: `"machine learning"`
5. **Field tags**: Search specific fields when available (title, abstract, author)

### Search Query Templates

| Purpose | Query Template |
|---------|---------------|
| Foundational papers | `"[topic]" AND "review" OR "survey"` |
| Recent advances | `"[topic]" AND [year-2]..[current_year]` |
| Methodology | `"[method name]" AND "[application domain]" |
| Gap identification | `"[topic]" AND "future work" OR "limitation"` |

### Source Selection

- **CrossRef**: Broadest coverage, all disciplines, best for DOI lookups
- **Semantic Scholar**: AI-powered relevance, good citation graph, best for CS/ML
- **PubMed**: Biomedical focus, MeSH terms, best for life sciences

## Evaluating Source Quality

### Journal Quality Indicators
- Impact factor / CiteScore
- Publisher reputation (Elsevier, Springer, Nature, IEEE, etc.)
- Indexing status (SCI, SSCI, Scopus, PubMed)

### Paper Quality Checklist
- [ ] Peer-reviewed?
- [ ] Sample size adequate?
- [ ] Methodology sound?
- [ ] Conclusions supported by data?
- [ ] Recent enough? (field-dependent: 3-10 years)
- [ ] Cited by others?

## Citation Management

### Placeholder Format
Use `[Author, Year]` in drafts. Examples:
- Single author: `[Smith, 2023]`
- Two authors: `[Smith & Jones, 2023]`
- Three+: `[Smith et al., 2023]`
- Multiple citations: `[Smith, 2023; Jones, 2022; Lee, 2021]`

### Citation Context Types
- **Supporting**: "Previous studies have shown [Author, Year]..."
- **Contrasting**: "However, [Author, Year] found that..."
- **Building**: "Building on the work of [Author, Year], we..."
- **Gap**: "Despite advances in X [Author, Year], Y remains unexplored."
