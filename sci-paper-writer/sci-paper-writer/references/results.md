# Results — Guided Drafting Module

## Purpose
Present findings objectively, without interpretation. Let the data speak.
Use figures, tables, and statistics to support every claim.

## Guiding Questions

### Main Findings
1. What are the 3-5 most important findings?
2. For each finding: what is the exact numerical result? (mean, CI, p-value, effect size)
3. Which findings were expected vs. surprising?

### Data Presentation
4. What figures best illustrate the key results? (bar chart, scatter, heatmap, etc.)
5. What tables are needed? (comparison table, summary statistics)
6. Are there supplementary results that support but don't belong in the main text?

### Statistical Results
7. What statistical tests were used for each comparison?
8. What are the exact p-values, confidence intervals, effect sizes?
9. Were multiple comparison corrections applied?

### Negative / Null Results
10. Were there expected effects that were NOT found?
11. Were there failed experiments or excluded datasets? (important for transparency)

### Subgroup / Sensitivity
12. Do results hold across subgroups?
13. Are there any sensitivity analyses to report?

## Structure Template

```
[Overview sentence: "We analyzed N = ... across M = ... conditions."]
[Result 1: Finding → evidence → figure/table reference]
[Result 2: Finding → evidence → figure/table reference]
[Result 3: ...]
[Negative/null results if relevant]
[Summary of key patterns]
```

## Writing Patterns

### Presenting a Finding
- "[Variable] was significantly [higher/lower] in [group] compared to [group] (M = X, SD = Y, t(df) = Z, p = .003)."
- "As shown in Figure N, [description of trend]."
- "Table N summarizes the [comparison] across [conditions]."
- "[Percentage] of [sample] exhibited [characteristic]."

### Referring to Figures/Tables
- "Figure N shows the relationship between [X] and [Y]."
- "As illustrated in Table N, [key observation]."
- "The distribution of [variable] is presented in Figure N."

### Reporting Statistics
- "The effect was statistically significant (β = X.XX, 95% CI [LL, UL], p < .001)."
- "A [test name] revealed a significant difference between groups (F(df1, df2) = X.XX, p = .XX)."
- "The correlation between [X] and [Y] was r = .XX, p = .XX."

### Null Results
- "No significant difference was observed between [groups] (p = .XX)."
- "[Variable] did not significantly predict [outcome] (β = X.XX, p = .XX)."

## Common Pitfalls
- Interpreting results (save for Discussion)
- Cherry-picking significant results while hiding null findings
- Presenting data without statistics (or vice versa)
- Figures that are hard to read (small fonts, unclear legends)
- Redundancy between text, tables, and figures (each should add new info)

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Every claim is backed by a number (mean, p-value, CI)
- [ ] Figures and tables are referenced in the text
- [ ] No interpretation or speculation (that's Discussion)
- [ ] Null/negative results are reported
- [ ] Statistics are complete (test statistic, df, p, effect size)
- [ ] Results section is self-contained (understandable without Discussion)
